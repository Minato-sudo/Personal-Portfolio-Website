## Overview

During his summer research internship at the Knowledge Discovery and Data Science (KDD) Lab at FAST-NUCES, Talha worked on multilingual and multi-prompt image generation systems built around diffusion-based models.

## What He Built

- Designed a custom `XLMRCLIPEncoder` that projected multilingual sentence embeddings into a 768-dimensional latent space so Stable Diffusion could process prompts across Urdu, Arabic, Chinese, Spanish, and English.
- Implemented a multi-prompt splitting workflow using regex and spaCy to separate complex prompt structures into controllable sub-prompts.
- Modified UNet cross-attention heads to support multi-prompt conditioning for richer scene generation.

## Training and Evaluation

- Trained models on COCO and Multi30k datasets at `256 x 256` resolution.
- Used A100 GPUs for experimentation and tracked training progress through Weights & Biases.
- Evaluated outputs with FID, IS, CLIPSim, and Semantic-Object-Accuracy style metrics.

## Deployment Work

Talha also contributed to an end-to-end unified repository that brought multilingual and multi-prompt pipelines together inside a Gradio web application. The deployment workflow was containerized with Docker to make experimentation and sharing easier.
