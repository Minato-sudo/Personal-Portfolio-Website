## Overview

This featured project brings together global disaster, climate, and governance datasets into one analytical framework for comparing national resilience. Talha fused four public datasets covering 78 variables and translated them into three composite indicators: Disaster Impact Index (DII), Resilience & Recovery Score (RRS), and Climate Risk Index (CRI).

## What Was Built

- A Python-based data preparation workflow for cleaning, aligning, and normalizing multi-source country-level data.
- A composite scoring framework that compares disaster impact against governance, climate pressure, and recovery capacity.
- Interactive Tableau dashboards that surface country-level trends, risk clusters, and recovery patterns.

## Key Insight

The resulting analysis suggested that governance quality had a stronger relationship with disaster recovery speed than GDP alone. That shifted the project from a descriptive dashboard into a more decision-oriented resilience framework.

## Stack

- Python
- Tableau
- Statistical Analysis
- Data Fusion

## Outcome

The framework produced a reusable structure for future resilience research and gave the final dashboard a clear narrative for policymakers, researchers, and academic reviewers.
