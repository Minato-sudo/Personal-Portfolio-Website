## Overview

The proliferation of online news sources has created severe information redundancy. This project presents a fully parallelized, three-stage pipeline for event-centric news clustering and storyline summarization. It successfully addresses a critical gap in modern NLP pipelines: processing efficiency at scale across tens of thousands of articles.

## What Was Built

- **Stage 1 (PDC Parallel Embedding):** A CPU multiprocessing architecture executing `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` embedding across distributed worker partitions.
- **Stage 2 (Temporal-Semantic Clustering):** A unified K-Means clustering model operating on 385-dimensional feature vectors that combine semantic density with normalized publication timestamps.
- **Stage 3 (GPU Abstractive Summarization):** A centroid-ranked text generation stage using `BART-large-CNN` on CUDA, condensing the most salient articles within a cluster into a coherent storyline.

## Why It Matters

Most NLP research evaluates accuracy while completely omitting computational efficiency constraints. By explicitly mapping tasks to optimal hardware components—distributing memory-bound embeddings to CPU worker threads while delegating heavy neural generation to CUDA tensor cores—the system achieves a 41% reduction in inference time (a 1.7x CPU speedup at 10,000 articles) without sacrificing normalized mutual information (NMI) clustering quality. 

## Stack

- Python Multiprocessing (PDC)
- PyTorch (CUDA)
- HuggingFace Transformers (BART, MiniLM)
- scikit-learn (K-Means)
- Sentence Embeddings

## Outcome

The project demonstrated quality-preserving scalability: NMI remained stable across 1k, 5k, and 10k dataset boundaries. Furthermore, GPU acceleration effectively collapsed the summarization stage from an unviable 6 hours sequentially down to just under 10 minutes, making the event-centric clustering engine practical for high-volume news ingestion deployments.
